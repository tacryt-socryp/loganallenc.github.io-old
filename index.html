Hi, I'm Logan Allen. I'm a driven young software developer living in Atlanta. A generalist, I have full-stack experience with multiple platforms. Design-oriented by nature, I craft streamlined apps with a focus on user experience.

A builder at heart, I'm a self-starter whose passion shows in everything I do. I love diving into new technologies and am able to quickly familiarize myself with large codebases to begin contributing.

I drink excessive amounts of coffee, run, and love music—mostly electronic and indie. I'm enrolled at the Georgia Institute of Technology as a Computer Science major.

2015

I recently co-founded Bitmo, a business that takes a new look at the idea of a virtual currency exchange. Bitmo allows you to invest in your Bitcoin wallet quickly with industry-leading systems and convert from Bitcoin back to US dollars just as fast.


I recently accepted an offer for a software engineering internship in San Francisco with the Web team at Uber for the summer.


2014

Pacekeeper is a hack developed at Hack The North that integrates with the heart rate monitor of the Moto360 smart watch with Android devices, and changes the tempo of the music accordingly. Pacekeeper utilizes Google Fit to record workout information.


Autoscope is a hack I created at MHacks IV that allows a person to view their life in third person. It was implemented using a parrot drone that streams video to a Rift while the person wearing the Rift is tracked and followed using optical flow tracking.

 

Autoscope was written entirely in WebGL and other open web technologies, and won the Best Under 20 Prize presented by the Thiel Foundation.


I co-founded Slingput, a tool for creating beautiful landing pages for gathering feedback and collecting payment. I developed Slingput in Ruby on Rails. Slingput ranked among the top five new startups on the front page of ProductHunt.


I worked as a software development intern at Gather. I've rewritten Gather's private events management web app for restaurants in AngularJS and created automated testing suites using Protractor. I also did a significant amount of work on writing backend services and workers.


I interned at TripLingo as a software engineer and used JavaScript and Appcelerator Titanium to reimagine their translation app for use in an emergency rooms translation context. I delved into the user experience of doctors and patients, and created a streamlined app for iPads.


2013

I co-founded Clerk in high school. Clerk is a news app that helps support writers through micro-donations. Our startup was advised by Pascal Finette, the past Chair of the Board of Mozilla.


I developed a medical app for BestFit Mobile as an independent contractor, acting as the lead front-end developer on an app called ReferMD for Fresenius Medical Care, a dialysis company with 55,000 employees.

 

I created a conference room booking app for tablets for internal use at BestFit Mobile using JavaScript and Sencha Touch.
